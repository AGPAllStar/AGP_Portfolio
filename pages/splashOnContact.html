Splash On Contact

  • Genres: Action-adventure
  • Engine: Unreal Engine 5.4
  • Tasks performed: Programming, System Design, Level Design, Game Design

Play the demo here

Game Description

This is an action-adventure game in which the player controls a Goteao called Splash who accompanies a Fertilizao called Splinter. During their journey, they will encounter a tribe of Fogosos who will try to kill Splinter, and it is the player’s duty to protect him. The player will progress through levels consisting of several interconnected rooms that combine exploration sections with dialogue and combat sections.

The player can eliminate enemies by colliding directly with them. Furthermore, the game uses a physics system that takes speed into account to determine the damage the player inflicts on enemies.

At the start of the game, our protagonists’ village is destroyed by a group of Fiery Ones; Splash’s master, Stick, is defeated, and Splash is forced to flee with Splinter. On their journey to safety, they will discover what has happened in the other villages and meet people who will help them along the way.

Tasks performed

Lead programmer

I was responsible for coordinating the game’s programming team, defining and assigning the tasks required for the game’s development.

Activity System

I developed a system that allows the gameplay context to be changed dynamically within a single Unreal Engine level. To achieve this, I grouped all the different gameplay contexts such as battles or dialogues into objects called "activities". Each activity is managed independently, defining the rules that apply, the actions the player can perform, and the information to be displayed on screen. The gameplay engine is responsible for changing the current activity and saving a reference to that activity.

Battles are organised in waves, during which a series of enemies appear at different points in the room. For each enemy that is due to appear, the enemy type, the time it would take to appear from the start of the wave, and the exact location where it would appear are stored. Until the player has eliminated all the enemies in a wave, they will not move on to the next one. The battle ends when the final wave is complete.

Dialogues are managed by dialogue lines, which display the message spoken by the speaker in the conversation between the characters. The dialogue displays the sprites of two characters, one of whom is the speaker of the current dialogue line.

Level Design

I designed the level shown in the published demo of the game. The level consists of several interconnected rooms which the player must explore to find the end of the level and guide Splinter safely there.

This is an introductory level designed to teach the player the main mechanics of the game and set the scene for the game’s story. The layout of the level’s rooms was designed to make it accessible to novice players and engaging for experienced players.

Testing

I have developed tools designed to help ensure the quality of the game. On the one hand, I have created a debug mode that allows you to view all the main characters’ attributes and the collisions of the elements that make up the map while the game is in progress. On the other hand, I have created a way of saving players’ games to a file so that they can be analysed later. The aim of this is to identify areas where the level could be improved.
